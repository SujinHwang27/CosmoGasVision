\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

This paper, in computer-vision language, is a sparse-view tomography problem with an unusual sensing modality. The volume to be reconstructed is the \textbf{intergalactic medium} (IGM) --- the diffuse gas between galaxies, which holds most of the universe's baryonic matter and forms the filaments and voids of the cosmic web. No camera images this volume directly. We observe it through \textbf{Lyman-alpha forest} spectra: light from a distant quasar crosses the IGM, neutral hydrogen along the path absorbs photons at a resonance wavelength, and cosmic-expansion Doppler shifts smear the absorption into a dense forest of narrow dips. Each 1D spectrum is a \textbf{sightline} --- one ray through the volume, the analogue of one CT scanline --- and the position of every dip encodes the gas state along that ray.

Reconstructing the 3D IGM from a sparse bundle of such 1D sightlines is therefore tomography from pencil-beam projections, known as \emph{Lyman-alpha tomography}~\cite{stark2015tomography} in astrophysics and as sparse-view inverse rendering with a known forward operator in CV vocabulary. Classical solutions TARDIS~\cite{horowitz2019tardis} and Wiener filtering~\cite{stark2015tomography} discretize the volume into a fixed voxel grid and succeed at $z \approx 2$--$3$, where the forest is information-rich. We study the low-redshift regime at $z = 0.3$, where the mean transmitted flux is $\langle F\rangle = \MeanFluxZpThree$ ($\approx 2\%$ absorption) and each pixel carries little signal.

\textbf{Motivation.} Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF)~\cite{mildenhall2020nerf} replaced voxel grids with a coordinate-conditioned MLP, supervised through a differentiable rendering integral. The IGM problem needs the same move. We are not rendering color; we are recovering a physical state from absorption profiles, and we want gradients that flow through the forward physics rather than through a discretization seam.

\textbf{Contribution.} We introduce \emph{CosmoGasVision}, a NeRF-style continuous field for cosmological gas coupled to a differentiable projector that maps the MLP-decoded fields to 1D Lyman-alpha optical depth (a log measure of light absorbed along the sightline; see \cref{sec:method}) via the analytic Voigt-Hjerting kernel~\cite{tepper2006voigt}. The contribution is the representation-plus-forward-model package: a continuous IGM field optimized end-to-end against the physics astrophysicists already trust, with sub-voxel recovered structures and a physically interpretable gradient path.

\textbf{What this paper is, and is not.} This paper does not demonstrate closed-loop 3D IGM tomography from sparse Ly$\alpha$ sightlines at $z = 0.3$. It characterizes the wall this method class hits at this regime. The production MLP fails two of the three directly-evaluated structure gates (the 1D flux power spectrum $P_F$ and the flux-PDF KS distance); the 3D density cross-correlation gate was never evaluated on the MLP in its defined form, because the MLP is supervised on 1D flux and produces no 3D density cube. The decisive evidence comes from an explicit-voxel-grid probe: a maximal-capacity-within-this-study free-per-voxel field ($\GridG^3$), trained under the same flux supervision through the same forward model, fits the observed flux about $\KTwoMarginFactor\times$ better than the true field does (data loss $\KTwoGridLoss$ vs.\ $\KTwoTruthLoss$, with a free amplitude on both) yet recovers only weak 3D structure ($\XiCeilingFraction$ of the field's own achievable cross-correlation ceiling). The flux through this forward model does not determine the 3D field: the inverse problem at $z = 0.3$ is under-constrained, and escaping the optimization collapse is necessary but not sufficient. We make no claim beyond $z = 0.3$.

\textbf{What this paper is.} (i) A continuous-neural-field formulation of Ly$\alpha$ tomography with a differentiable RSD-convolved Voigt integrator and a mean-flux anchor at the verified low-$z$ value~\cite{kirkman2007}; (ii) a falsification-disciplined methodology of pre-committed structure gates, degeneracy-aware counterfactuals, and pre-committed stop conditions, demonstrated across $\NumInterventions$ retired interventions; (iii) an explicit-grid degeneracy measurement that localizes and bounds the failure mode --- the flux inverse problem is under-constrained at $z = 0.3$ under this forward model, scoped to this regime and not generalized.

The canonical classical IGM-tomography baselines are TARDIS~\cite{horowitz2019tardis} (Bayesian Wiener-filter reconstruction) and Stark+ Wiener filtering~\cite{stark2015tomography}. We position the continuous-neural-field formulation against these as qualitative comparison; numerical reproduction of either is out of scope.
