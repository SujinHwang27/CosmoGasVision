\section{Next Steps}
\label{sec:nextsteps}

The binding gate at the corrected anchor and full publication schedule is $P_F$: mean-flux and KS close while $|\Delta P_F/P_F|$ remains uniformly $\PFfactorOverGateLo\times$--$\PFfactorOverGateHi\times$ over the $\GatePFdec$ gate (\S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval}). The failure is structural rather than calibration, and log-space $\tau$-MSE is not a sufficient proxy for it. The $\tau$-binned residual decomposition (\S\ref{sec:experiments:tau-binned}) positively identifies the saturation band $F\!\in\![\SatBandLo, \SatBandHi]$ as the residually-supported mechanism cross-physics ($R \in [\RsatRangeLo, \RsatRangeHi]$, $> \!\RsatFactorOverFloor\times$ floor in $4/4$ cells). Four counterfactual interventions on that axis were tested under pre-committed stop conditions; all four retired empirically. We summarize them below as evidence for the successor-design constraints they leave behind, not as an open ranking.

\subsection{Four retired counterfactual interventions on the saturation-band axis}
\label{sec:nextsteps:pf}
Each entry below was specified before its run, executed under a pre-committed stop condition, and retired at the indicated tier when the empirical signature did not match the designed minimum. The four failures share a structural cause: any regularizer evaluated only on the network's self-consistent state is gameable when the data loss is weakly informative on the diffuse-bin majority, because constant-field collapse trivially satisfies any algebraic relation among the predicted fields --- a shortcut-learning mode in the sense of Geirhos et al.~\cite{geirhos2020shortcut}.

\textbf{1.\ Saturation-aware $P_F$ band loss --- retired.} An integrated relative-residual band loss $((P_{F,\text{pred}}-P_{F,\text{truth}})/P_{F,\text{truth}})^2$ was tested on $P_1$ at the cost-survey schedule ($n_{\text{rays}}=64$, $12{,}500$ steps, seed $=0$, anchor $\AnchorMeanF$). Eval $|\Delta P_F/P_F|=\PFdfortyEval$ ($+\PFdfortyEvalDelta$ worse than the \texttt{pub-t1} $P_1$ baseline of $\PFresidPone$); KS gate broke ($\KSdfortyEval$). Degeneracy class: amplitude-shrink with shape preservation ($P_{F,\text{pred}}\!\approx\!0.43\,P_{F,\text{truth}}$ uniformly), a constant-prediction minimum of the integrated relative-residual formulation when $P_{F,\text{truth}}$ is small in any band.

\textbf{2.\ FGPA-tail per-pixel regularizer --- retired.} A per-pixel Huber penalty on the FGPA scaling $\log\tau \propto \beta\log(\rho/\bar\rho)+\gamma\log T$~\cite{bi_davidsen1997,hui_gnedin1997} (Hui--Gnedin $\beta\!=\!1.6$, $\gamma\!=\!-0.7$) was tested at smoke ($P_1$, 50 steps) and confirmed at Tier-1 ($25{,}000$ steps, same configuration). The training-time regularizer descended $20\times$ ($6.37 \to 0.31$), but eval $|\Delta P_F/P_F| = 1.000$ ($\sim\!10\times$ worse than baseline) and the FGPA-valid input fields collapsed to near-uniform constants on the Tier-1 checkpoint (density $\approx 71.5$, $X_{HI}\!\approx\!3.3\!\times\!10^{-5}$). Degeneracy class: constant-prediction collapse on a self-consistent state --- constants $(\rho, T)$ trivially satisfy the FGPA single-equation constraint, and the mean-flux anchor pulls the resulting constant $\tau$ to mean-$F\!\approx\!1$.

\textbf{3.\ Velocity-gradient conditioning branch --- retired at smoke.} A conditioning branch on the observation-derived $\partial v_{\text{pec}}/\partial \chi$ along the line of sight was injected into the density head, chosen specifically to break the self-consistent-state degeneracy of \#2 (ground-truth-anchored, not network-predicted). The 50-step $P_1$ smoke passed gates 1--4 and 6 but failed gate 5 (predicted-density spread $0.0071$ vs.\ $\geq 1.45$ bar, $200\times$ below floor); the density head collapsed to $\approx 0$ while the $X_{HI}$ head retained structure. Degeneracy class: partial-collapse signature distinct from \#1 (integrated-amplitude shrink) and \#2 (full self-consistent collapse). Retired per pre-committed stop condition without Tier-1 dispatch.

\textbf{4.\ Joint-physics conditional MLP with physics\_id embedding --- retired at smoke.} A 16-dim physics\_id embedding was concatenated into the MLP input to train one network jointly on sightlines pooled across $\{P_1, P_2, P_3, P_4\}$ (4096 sightlines, 1024 per physics, microbatch interleaving), targeting the conditioning axis (cross-physics pooling via an added conditioning input) orthogonal to the loss/regularizer/architecture-input axes of \#1--\#3. The 50-step P-mixed smoke passed gates 1, 4, 6, 7 but failed gates 2, 3, 5: loss-flat (ratio $1.0036$ vs.\ $<0.85$ required), per-physics mean-$F\!=\!1.000$ in all four cells (trivial-flux collapse), and per-physics predicted-density spread $6.4\!\times\!10^{-5}$ to $2.0\!\times\!10^{-4}$ ($\sim\!10{,}000\times$ below the $1.45$ floor). The embedding itself was non-degenerate (gate 7 PASS, max pairwise $L_2 = 7.045$ in 16-dim space); the network learned 4 distinct codes yet routed all of them into the same trivial-flux density-collapse solution. Degeneracy class: combined-trivial-collapse-with-active-embedding, distinct from \#1 (amplitude-shrink), \#2 (self-consistent constant collapse), and \#3 (asymmetric-head partial collapse), and notable for reproducing the constant-field-collapse mechanism of \#1/\#2 even when the conditioning surface is non-degenerate.

The residually-supported (c) mechanism --- saturation-band under-fitting cross-physics --- remains positively identified but not closed-loop. New interventions are out of scope for this paper; a successor design would need to encode a constraint against constant-field collapse that does not rely on the network's own predicted fields for its anchor, even with non-degenerate physics\_id conditioning (a falsification-discipline rule banked from this sprint).

\subsection{Tier 4 and sightline-density scaling}
\label{sec:nextsteps:tier4}
Tier 4 (sightline density $n_{\text{rays}}=16{,}384$, $\sim\!\GPUHoursTierFour$ GPU-hours across four feedback variants) is the natural completion of the sightline-density $\times$ physics-feedback degradation matrix (see suppl.) and the unlock for the full 3D $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}(r)$ gate. With the $\tau$-binned decomposition (\S\ref{sec:experiments:tau-binned}) positively identifying saturation-regime under-fitting cross-physics, the $\sim\!\GPUHoursTierFour$-GPU-hr budget would not be expected to close $P_F$ on sightline density alone.

The four feedback variants of the \texttt{pub-t1} bundle span the small-scale gas thermodynamics range covered by Sherwood, yet at fixed $n_{\text{rays}}=64$ all four cells fail $P_F$ uniformly at $\PFfactorOverGateLo\times$--$\PFfactorOverGateHi\times$ over the $\GatePFdec$ gate, with $R \in [\RsatRangeLo, \RsatRangeHi]$ in $4/4$ cells (cross-physics bootstrap-confirmed; suppl.). $N\!=\!4$ interventions on loss, regularizer, architecture-input, and conditioning axes cataloged $N\!=\!4$ distinct degeneracy signatures, all reachable from the log1p$+$cap$+$mask supervision regime with the Kirkman~\cite{kirkman2007} $\langle F\rangle$ anchor --- the reading we draw is underspecification of that supervision target~\cite{damour2020underspecification} rather than a closed enumeration of intervention space. The supervision-target axis itself remains an open and arguably-upstream axis not exercised by these four interventions, and a cascade that fails through one upstream filter does not foreclose that filter as a candidate~\cite{manheim_garrabrant2018}. These claims are scoped to the Sherwood Suite at $z=0.3$; cross-suite (Nyx, IllustrisTNG) and multi-redshift extension is the natural next test. Stage 3 ($\rho$-crop classifier) is out of scope, gated on a $P_F$-passing reconstruction. Decision log: suppl.

\subsection{Substrate-scale follow-on}
\label{sec:nextsteps:substrate}
The substrate-discriminability probe (\S\ref{sec:experiments:substrate-probe}) rules out the $\SubstrateProbeCropSize^3$ truth-$\rho$ crop as a substrate carrying cross-physics signal beyond two summary moments, and makes three axes load-bearing for any successor probe. (a) \textbf{Substrate scale}: coarser crops or larger fields of view may recover the $P_2$/$P_3$ separation that the present probe loses; finer features (e.g., temperature, $X_{HI}$, or peculiar-velocity gradients) may carry residual signal $\rho$ does not. (b) \textbf{Supervision-target alternatives}: the same upstream-untested axis identified in \S\ref{sec:nextsteps:pf} also bears on the substrate probe, since the substrate the probe sees on a reconstructed field will inherit any structural bias of the supervision regime~\cite{manheim_garrabrant2018}. (c) \textbf{Reconstructed-substrate evaluation}: the present probe was run on truth $\rho$; whether the $\sim\!1$ pp margin survives or widens on a reconstructed substrate is an open empirical question and the direct extension of this work toward a quantitative reconstruction-fidelity audit.
