\section{Next Steps}
\label{sec:nextsteps}

The binding gate at the corrected anchor and full publication schedule is $P_F$: mean-flux and KS close while $|\Delta P_F/P_F|$ remains uniformly $\PFfactorOverGateLo\times$--$\PFfactorOverGateHi\times$ over the $\GatePFdec$ gate (\S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval}). The failure is structural rather than calibration, and log-space $\tau$-MSE is not a sufficient proxy for it. The $\tau$-binned residual decomposition (\S\ref{sec:experiments:tau-binned}) positively identifies the saturation band $F\!\in\![\SatBandLo, \SatBandHi]$ as the residually-supported mechanism cross-physics ($R \in [\RsatRangeLo, \RsatRangeHi]$, $> \!\RsatFactorOverFloor\times$ floor in $4/4$ cells). Three counterfactual interventions on that axis were tested under pre-committed stop conditions; all three retired empirically. We summarize them below as evidence for the successor-design constraints they leave behind, not as an open ranking.

\subsection{Three retired counterfactual interventions on the saturation-band axis}
\label{sec:nextsteps:pf}
Each entry below was specified before its run, executed under a pre-committed stop condition, and retired at the indicated tier when the empirical signature did not match the designed minimum. The three failures share a structural cause: any regularizer evaluated only on the network's self-consistent state is gameable when the data loss is weakly informative on the diffuse-bin majority, because constant-field collapse trivially satisfies any algebraic relation among the predicted fields.

\textbf{1.\ Saturation-aware $P_F$ band loss --- retired ([D-40]).} An integrated relative-residual band loss $((P_{F,\text{pred}}-P_{F,\text{truth}})/P_{F,\text{truth}})^2$ was tested on $P_1$ at the cost-survey schedule ($n_{\text{rays}}=64$, $12{,}500$ steps, seed $=0$, anchor $\AnchorMeanF$). Eval $|\Delta P_F/P_F|=\PFdfortyEval$ ($+\PFdfortyEvalDelta$ worse than the \texttt{pub-t1} $P_1$ baseline of $\PFresidPone$); KS gate broke ($\KSdfortyEval$). Degeneracy class: amplitude-shrink with shape preservation ($P_{F,\text{pred}}\!\approx\!0.43\,P_{F,\text{truth}}$ uniformly), a constant-prediction minimum of the integrated relative-residual formulation when $P_{F,\text{truth}}$ is small in any band.

\textbf{2.\ FGPA-tail per-pixel regularizer --- retired ([D-41]).} A per-pixel Huber penalty on the FGPA scaling $\log\tau \propto \beta\log(\rho/\bar\rho)+\gamma\log T$~\cite{bi_davidsen1997,hui_gnedin1997} (Hui--Gnedin $\beta\!=\!1.6$, $\gamma\!=\!-0.7$) was tested at smoke ($P_1$, 50 steps) and confirmed at Tier-1 ($25{,}000$ steps, same configuration). The training-time regularizer descended $20\times$ ($6.37 \to 0.31$), but eval $|\Delta P_F/P_F| = 1.000$ ($\sim\!10\times$ worse than baseline) and the FGPA-valid input fields collapsed to near-uniform constants on the Tier-1 checkpoint (density $\approx 71.5$, $X_{HI}\!\approx\!3.3\!\times\!10^{-5}$). Degeneracy class: constant-prediction collapse on a self-consistent state --- constants $(\rho, T)$ trivially satisfy the FGPA single-equation constraint, and the mean-flux anchor pulls the resulting constant $\tau$ to mean-$F\!\approx\!1$.

\textbf{3.\ Velocity-gradient conditioning branch --- retired at smoke ([D-42]).} A conditioning branch on the observation-derived $\partial v_{\text{pec}}/\partial \chi$ along the line of sight was injected into the density head, chosen specifically to break the self-consistent-state degeneracy of \#2 (ground-truth-anchored, not network-predicted). The 50-step $P_1$ smoke passed gates 1--4 and 6 but failed gate 5 (predicted-density spread $0.0071$ vs.\ $\geq 1.45$ bar, $200\times$ below floor); the density head collapsed to $\approx 0$ while the $X_{HI}$ head retained structure. Degeneracy class: partial-collapse signature distinct from \#1 (integrated-amplitude shrink) and \#2 (full self-consistent collapse). Retired per pre-committed stop condition without Tier-1 dispatch.

The residually-supported (c) mechanism --- saturation-band under-fitting cross-physics --- remains positively identified but not closed-loop. New interventions are out of scope for this paper; the successor design must encode a constraint against constant-field collapse that does not rely on the network's own predicted fields for its anchor (an [D-37]-extension rule banked from this sprint).

\subsection{Tier 4 and sightline-density scaling}
\label{sec:nextsteps:tier4}
Tier 4 (sightline density $n_{\text{rays}}=16{,}384$, $\sim\!\GPUHoursTierFour$ GPU-hours across four feedback variants) is the natural completion of the sightline-density $\times$ physics-feedback degradation matrix (see suppl.) and the unlock for the full 3D $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}(r)$ gate. With the $\tau$-binned decomposition (\S\ref{sec:experiments:tau-binned}) positively identifying saturation-regime under-fitting cross-physics, the $\sim\!\GPUHoursTierFour$-GPU-hr budget would not be expected to close $P_F$ on sightline density alone.

The argument-from-invariance bears explicit unpacking. The four feedback variants of the \texttt{pub-t1} bundle --- $P_1$ (no feedback), $P_2$ (winds), $P_3$ (winds$+$AGN), $P_4$ (strong AGN) --- span the full range of small-scale gas thermodynamics in Sherwood, yet at fixed $n_{\text{rays}}=64$ all four cells fail $P_F$ at the same $\PFfactorOverGateLo\times$--$\PFfactorOverGateHi\times$ over the $\GatePFdec$ gate, with $R \in [\RsatRangeLo, \RsatRangeHi]$ in $4/4$ cells ([D-39], cross-physics bootstrap-confirmed under [D-44]). Sightline density modulates the \emph{sampling} of an underlying field, not the field's \emph{form}; a saturation-band representation deficit physics-invariant at $n_{\text{rays}}=64$ is unlikely to be addressed by adding samples. The retired counterfactuals of \S\ref{sec:nextsteps:pf} point to the loss / regularizer / architecture axis. Stage 3 ($\rho$-crop classifier) is out of scope, gated on a $P_F$-passing reconstruction.
