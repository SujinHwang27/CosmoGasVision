\begin{abstract}
Reconstructing the 3D distribution of the intergalactic medium (IGM) from sparse 1D Lyman-alpha forest sightlines is a sparse-view tomography problem with a physically derived forward operator. Classical voxel-grid methods such as TARDIS~\cite{horowitz2019tardis} and Wiener filtering~\cite{stark2015tomography} succeed at $z \approx 2$--$3$, where the forest is information-rich. We study the low-redshift regime at $z = 0.3$, where the mean transmitted flux is $\langle F\rangle = \MeanFluxZpThree$ ($\approx 2\%$ absorption) and the per-pixel signal is sparse.

We present \emph{CosmoGasVision}, a continuous neural field $F_\theta:\mathbf{x} \to (\rho, T, X_{HI}, v_{\text{pec}})$ optimized end-to-end against a differentiable Lyman-alpha optical-depth integrator. The integrator implements the Tepper-Garc\'ia~\cite{tepper2006voigt} analytic Voigt-Hjerting kernel with full redshift-space-distortion (RSD) convolution and a learnable amplitude, and supervises the field on 1D flux through the [D-24] log-space loss with a mean-flux anchor at $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}}(z=0.3) = \AnchorMeanF$~\cite{kirkman2007}.

The contribution is a characterization of the optimization and identifiability wall this method class hits at $z = 0.3$. The decisive evidence is an explicit-voxel-grid probe: a maximal-capacity-within-this-study free-per-voxel field ($\GridG^3$), trained under the same flux supervision through the same forward model, closes the flux-power gate the production MLP failed ($|\Delta P_F/P_F| = \PFresidGrid$ vs.\ $\PFresidGridBaseline$) and fits the observed flux about $\KTwoMarginFactor\times$ better than the \emph{true} field does (data loss $\KTwoGridLoss$ vs.\ $\KTwoTruthLoss$, free amplitude on both), yet recovers only weak 3D structure. The grid trains --- its predicted-flux-power-band variance is $\VarPfBandRatio$, flat over training --- so it escapes the optimization collapse that retired earlier interventions, but its density cross-correlation at $r = 2\,h^{-1}$ Mpc reaches only $\XiCeilingFraction$ of the field's own achievable ceiling. The reading is that the $z = 0.3$ flux inverse problem, under this forward model, is under-constrained: escaping the optimization collapse is necessary but not sufficient, because the flux through this integrator does not determine the 3D field. We do not separate problem-intrinsic from forward-model-induced under-determination, and we make no claim beyond $z = 0.3$. The contributions are (i) a continuous-neural-field formulation of Ly$\alpha$ tomography coupled to a differentiable Voigt integrator; (ii) a falsification-disciplined methodology of pre-committed gates and degeneracy-aware counterfactuals, demonstrated across $\NumInterventions$ retired interventions; and (iii) the explicit-grid degeneracy measurement that localizes and bounds the failure mode at this regime.
\end{abstract}
