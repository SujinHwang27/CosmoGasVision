\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Dataset}
We use the \textbf{Sherwood Simulation Suite}~\cite{bolton2017sherwood} 60 Mpc/h Planck-cosmology volume at $2048^3$ resolution; the dev redshift is $z = 0.3$. Each snapshot ships 16{,}384 sightlines along a single axis at 2048 velocity bins per ray, with ground-truth $\tau_{HI}(v)$ stored as \texttt{tauH1\_2048\_n16384\_z0.300.dat}. Sherwood ships four feedback variants: $P_1$ (no feedback), $P_2$ (stellar wind), $P_3$ (wind $+$ AGN), $P_4$ (wind $+$ strong AGN), an ablation matrix over baryonic feedback prescriptions. We train one independent field per variant so a downstream feedback-classification probe cannot leak the label through a conditioning input. We report Tiers 1--3 across all four variants; Tier 4 is deferred per the compute argument of \S\ref{sec:experiments:compute}.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
Three primary [D-13] structure gates are computed against the simulation ground truth on the rendered $F=e^{-\tau}$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{1D flux power spectrum} $P_F(k_\parallel)$ (Hann-windowed periodogram, $dv/\sum w^2$ leakage compensation): $|\Delta P_F / P_F| < \GatePF$ averaged over $k_\parallel \in [10^{\PFkLoExp}, 10^{\PFkHiExp}]$ s/km~\cite{walther2018powerspectrum, boera2019thermal}.
    \item \textbf{Density-density cross-correlation} $\xi_{\hat\rho, \rho}(r)$ (Pearson on $\delta=\rho/\bar\rho-1$, FFT cross-power, periodic 60 Mpc/h grid, spherical-shell binning): $\xi_{\hat\rho, \rho}(r{=}2\,h^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}) > \GateXi$ at fiducial~\cite{lee2015, stark2015tomography}.
    \item \textbf{Flux PDF}: two-sample KS distance on $F$ restricted to $F \in [\PDFwinLo, \PDFwinHi]$ (lower cut excludes saturated absorbers; upper cut excludes continuum/metal-line residuals near $F\approx 1$~\cite{lee2015}): KS $< \GateKS$.
\end{itemize}
The fiducial pass point is $P_1$, $z = 0.3$, $n_{\text{rays}} = 1024$. The differentiable integrator was re-validated end-to-end at production architecture on the AWS SageMaker \texttt{ml.g5.xlarge} loop with S3 checkpoint sync; peak VRAM at the smallest tier is $\TonePeakVRAM$ GB, the anchor for the per-tier microbatch table of \S\ref{sec:method}.

\subsection{Stage 2b cost-survey: [D-34] mean-flux anchor retraction}
\label{sec:experiments:cost-survey-sweep}
The Stage 2b cost-survey production sweep ran the per-tier schedule of \S\ref{sec:method} across all four feedback variants at Tiers 1--3 (12 banked cells, all PASS the [D-19] safety rails; \texttt{peak\_vram\_gb} identical to four decimals across physics within each tier, empirically confirming the [D-23] linear-VRAM model). Micro-grid calibration and per-tier numerics are in the supplementary. The Stage 2b runs were trained against $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}} = 0.877$ at $z=0.3$. Phase~A.1 literature audit found this value corresponds to a $z\!\sim\!2$ mean transmitted flux mislabeled as $z=0.3$ during project history; the verified low-redshift value is $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}}(z=0.3) = \AnchorMeanF \pm \AnchorMeanFsigma$~\cite{kirkman2007}, retracted as anchor target in [D-34]. The [D-13] structure gates ($P_F$ inertial-range residual, KS-PDF distance, $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}$) are anchor-invariant by construction (invariant to uniform multiplicative rescaling of $\hat F$), so the cost-survey calibration evidence is unaffected. A separate corrected-anchor full-schedule run (\texttt{pub-t1}, $50{,}000$ steps, four physics, seed $=0$) trained directly against $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}}=\AnchorMeanF$ and recovered $\langle F_{\text{pred}}\rangle \in [\meanFseedFortyTwoRangeLo, \meanFseedFortyTwoRangeHi]$ in $4/4$ cells with $\tau_{\text{amp}} \in [\tauampPubtoneRangeLo, \tauampPubtoneRangeHi]$. The [D-13] structure-gate readout on those checkpoints is in \S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval} (per-cell values in suppl.). A $\tau_{\max}$ sensitivity gate ($\tau_{\max}\in\{5,10,20\}$, paired P1-T1 cells) closed at $\sim\!0.01$--$0.02\%$ vs.\ the $\TauMaxSensGate$ gate bar; $\tau_{\max}=10$ is locked (suppl.).

\subsection{Cosmological evaluation: $P_F$ falsifies the rescale-preview reading}
\label{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval}
The three [D-13] structure gates ($P_F(k_\parallel)$, $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}(r)$, KS-distance on the $F$-PDF) are evaluated at two checkpoints: (i) the Tier-3 cost-survey checkpoint at step $10{,}000$, the data available before the corrected-anchor run, and (ii) the Tier-1 corrected-anchor full-schedule \texttt{pub-t1} run at step $50{,}000$ across all four feedback variants ([D-39], MLflow runs \texttt{31acdf9d\dots} / \texttt{f7fafa23\dots} / \texttt{62aeb93a\dots} / \texttt{fc3817b3\dots} for $P_1$/$P_2$/$P_3$/$P_4$). Two flux-domain gates ($P_F$, $F$-PDF) are evaluated directly on each checkpoint; the line-of-sight density axis is reported via the [D-33] 1D-along-ray proxy (per-sightline Pearson $r$ on $\log_{10}\rho/\langle\rho\rangle$ at integrator quadrature points), a necessary-but-not-sufficient surrogate for the full 3D $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}(r)$.

\textbf{Rescale-as-preview, retracted ([D-39]).} The cost-survey rescale-as-preview interpretation ([D-34]/[D-35]) is empirically falsified by the trained-at-target $P_F$ residual (overlay figure in suppl.): $4\times$ more training and the corrected anchor increased $|\Delta P_F/P_F|$ from $\PFrescaledPercent$ (rescaled preview) to $\PFresidPonePercent$ at $P_1$.

\textbf{Empirical observation and diagnosis: $P_F$ is structurally bound, not calibration-bound.} At the corrected anchor and full publication schedule (per-cell values in suppl.), the four-physics bundle at the seed=42 single-seed anchor achieves: $\langle F_{\text{pred}}\rangle \in [\meanFseedFortyTwoRangeLo, \meanFseedFortyTwoRangeHi]$ in $4/4$ cells (cross-physics spread $\meanFseedFortyTwoSpread$, $<\!1\%$ bar); KS$\,< \GateKS$ in $3/4$ cells (range $[\KSseedFortyTwoPone, \KSseedFortyTwoPtwo]$, $P_2$ the single breach); [D-19] safety $4/4$; \texttt{peak\_vram}$=\peakVRAMPubtone$ GB; \texttt{tau\_amp}$\in[\tauampPubtoneRangeLo, \tauampPubtoneRangeHi]$; and $|\Delta P_F/P_F| \in [\PFresidLo, \PFresidHi]$ over $k_\parallel \in [10^{\PFkLoExp}, 10^{\PFkHiExp}]$ s/km in $4/4$ cells, $\PFfactorOverGateLo\times$--$\PFfactorOverGateHi\times$ over the $\GatePFdec$ gate. The [D-44] 5-seed sightline-level bootstrap returns $\langle F_{\rm pred}\rangle \in [\meanFBootstrapRangeLo, \meanFBootstrapRangeHi]$ (cross-physics spread $\meanFBootstrapSpread$, systematically shifted below the seed=42 anchor by $\meanFseedDeviationRangeLo$--$\meanFseedDeviationRangeHi\sigma$); KS closes $3/4$ bootstrap-robustly. The conjunction --- mean-flux closure at the single-seed anchor, near-uniform KS closure, and a uniformly $\sim\!4\times$-over $P_F$ residual --- is inconsistent with a calibration deficit (which would shift mean-flux without preserving the $\meanFBootstrapSpread$ cross-physics tightness) and with a $P_2$-only seed-collapse story (which would not produce $P_F$ residuals at the three non-collapsed cells). The binding gap is in second-order spectroscopic statistics that survive matched mean-flux and matched flux PDF.

\textbf{Decoupling finding.} Log-space $\tau$-MSE is not a sufficient proxy for the [D-13] spectroscopic gates; see suppl.

The single-seed seed=42 anchor values for all four cells, the $P_F$-overlay falsification figure, the cost-survey-checkpoint companion plots, and the [D-13] three-checkpoint comparison table are in the supplementary.

\subsection{$\tau$-binned residual decomposition: saturation-regime under-fitting, positively identified cross-physics}
\label{sec:experiments:tau-binned}
The structural-$P_F$ verdict of \S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval} states \emph{that} the gap is in second-order spectroscopic statistics and \emph{not} in calibration. The diagnostic question is \emph{where on the $F$ axis} the gap lives. We answer this with a $\tau$-binned residual decomposition (\texttt{scripts/tau\_binned\_residual.py --cell all}) over the four \texttt{pub-t1} corrected-anchor checkpoints at step $50{,}000$, seed $=42$, $1024$ rays. The diagnostic partitions the rendered $F$ axis into seven bins matching the [D-24] saturated-absorber definition --- linear regime $F > 0.9$ (bins 1--4, $\sim\!99.6\%$ of pixels), transition band, and saturation band $F \in [\SatBandLo, \SatBandHi]$ (bin 7) --- and reports the per-bin residual mass $\sum |\Delta F|$ alongside pixel share. The concentration ratio $R = (\text{residual-mass share}) / (\text{pixel-mass share})$ measures how much of the per-sightline error budget the saturation band absorbs relative to its size.

\textbf{Headline.} The saturation-band $R$ is $\{\RsatPone, \RsatPtwo, \RsatPthree, \RsatPfour\}$ across $P_1$/$P_2$/$P_3$/$P_4$ (per-cell breakdown and 4-panel residual-mass histograms in suppl.). All four cells exceed the positive-ID floor $R \geq \GateRsatFloor$ by $> \!\RsatFactorOverFloor\times$, the cross-physics spread is $\sim\!\RsatSpreadRelative$ relative, and $P_4$ (strongest-feedback variant, where cool dense structure is most prevalent) has the largest $R$. The linear regime contributes $|\langle \Delta F\rangle| \leq \RsatLinearBandResidual$ across all four cells, so the cross-physics flux residual budget is dominated by the saturation band rather than spread across the $F$ axis. Within the saturation band, the per-bin Pearson $r$ on $\tau$ between predicted and ground-truth values is $r_{\text{pearson}}(\tau) = \RsatPearsonTauPone$ at $P_1$: the $\tau$-rank order collapses where it matters most for $P_F$, ruling out a global $\tau$ rescaling (uniform $\tau \to s \cdot \tau$ preserves rank order in-band) as a sufficient fix.

\textbf{Interpretation under [D-37].} The decomposition positively identifies the saturation regime as the residually-supported mechanism cross-physics. We do not claim closure of the inferential gap: the strict ``saturation under-fitting \emph{is the cause} of the $P_F$ gap'' claim is reserved for the successor counterfactual run (\S\ref{sec:nextsteps:pf}). The current claim ceiling is \emph{positively identified as the residually-supported mechanism cross-physics; counterfactual closure (saturation-aware loss) deferred to successor work}.

The full-resolution per-physics breakdown (4-panel residual-mass histograms with saturation-band shading) is provided as Fig.~3 of the supplementary. The per-cell $R$ values are tabulated there alongside the figure.
