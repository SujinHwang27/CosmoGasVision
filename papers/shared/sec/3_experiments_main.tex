\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Dataset}
We use the \textbf{Sherwood Simulation Suite}~\cite{bolton2017sherwood} 60 Mpc/h Planck-cosmology volume at $2048^3$ resolution; the dev redshift is $z = 0.3$. Each snapshot ships 16{,}384 sightlines along a single axis at 2048 velocity bins per ray, with ground-truth $\tau_{HI}(v)$ stored as \texttt{tauH1\_2048\_n16384\_z0.300.dat}. Sherwood ships four feedback variants: $P_1$ (no feedback), $P_2$ (stellar wind), $P_3$ (wind $+$ AGN), $P_4$ (wind $+$ strong AGN), an ablation matrix over baryonic feedback prescriptions. We train one independent field per variant so a downstream feedback-classification probe cannot leak the label through a conditioning input. We report Tiers 1--3 across all four variants; Tier 4 is deferred per the compute argument of \S\ref{sec:experiments:compute}.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
Three primary structure gates are computed against the simulation ground truth on the rendered $F=e^{-\tau}$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{1D flux power spectrum} $P_F(k_\parallel)$ (Hann-windowed periodogram, $dv/\sum w^2$ leakage compensation): $|\Delta P_F / P_F| < \GatePF$ averaged over $k_\parallel \in [10^{\PFkLoExp}, 10^{\PFkHiExp}]$ s/km~\cite{walther2018powerspectrum, boera2019thermal}.
    \item \textbf{Density-density cross-correlation} $\xi_{\hat\rho, \rho}(r)$ --- a Stark-style FFT cross-power, normalized by each field's global std and inverse-transformed, on the periodic 60 Mpc/h grid~\cite{lee2015, stark2015tomography}. The $\xi(r{=}2\,h^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}) > \GateXi$ bar is \emph{demoted} below: the estimator returns a cross-correlation \emph{function} that decays with $r$, not a per-lag coefficient, so even truth-vs-truth scores only $\XiCeiling$ at $r = 2\,h^{-1}$ Mpc and no field can reach $\GateXi$. We report $\xi$ ceiling-relative.
    \item \textbf{Flux PDF}: two-sample KS distance on $F$ restricted to $F \in [\PDFwinLo, \PDFwinHi]$ (lower cut excludes saturated absorbers; upper cut excludes continuum/metal-line residuals near $F\approx 1$~\cite{lee2015}): KS $< \GateKS$.
\end{itemize}
The fiducial pass point is $P_1$, $z = 0.3$, $n_{\text{rays}} = 1024$. The differentiable integrator was re-validated end-to-end at production architecture on the AWS SageMaker \texttt{ml.g5.xlarge} loop with S3 checkpoint sync; peak VRAM at the smallest tier is $\TonePeakVRAM$ GB, the anchor for the per-tier microbatch table of \S\ref{sec:method}.

\subsection{Stage 2b cost-survey: mean-flux anchor retraction}
\label{sec:experiments:cost-survey-sweep}
The Stage 2b cost-survey production sweep ran the per-tier schedule of \S\ref{sec:method} across all four feedback variants at Tiers 1--3 (12 banked cells, all PASS pre-registered safety rails; \texttt{peak\_vram\_gb} identical to four decimals across physics within each tier, empirically confirming a linear-VRAM scaling model). Micro-grid calibration and per-tier numerics are in the supplementary. The Stage 2b runs were trained against $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}} = 0.877$ at $z=0.3$. Phase~A.1 literature audit found this value corresponds to a $z\!\sim\!2$ mean transmitted flux mislabeled as $z=0.3$ during project history; the verified low-redshift value is $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}}(z=0.3) = \AnchorMeanF \pm \AnchorMeanFsigma$~\cite{kirkman2007}, retracted as anchor target. The structure gates ($P_F$ inertial-range residual, KS-PDF distance, $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}$) are anchor-invariant by construction (invariant to uniform multiplicative rescaling of $\hat F$), so the cost-survey calibration evidence is unaffected. A separate corrected-anchor full-schedule run (\texttt{pub-t1}, $50{,}000$ steps, four physics, seed $=0$) trained against $\langle F\rangle_{\text{obs}}=\AnchorMeanF$; the structure-gate readout is in \S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval}. The $\tau_{\max}$ sensitivity gate cleared at $\sim\!0.01$--$0.02\%$ (suppl.); $\tau_{\max}=10$ is locked.

\subsection{Cosmological evaluation: $P_F$ falsifies the rescale-preview reading}
\label{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval}
The three structure gates ($P_F(k_\parallel)$, $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}(r)$, KS-distance on the $F$-PDF) are evaluated at two checkpoints: (i) the Tier-3 cost-survey checkpoint at step $10{,}000$, the data available before the corrected-anchor run, and (ii) the Tier-1 corrected-anchor full-schedule \texttt{pub-t1} run at step $50{,}000$ across all four feedback variants (MLflow runs \texttt{31acdf9d\dots} / \texttt{f7fafa23\dots} / \texttt{62aeb93a\dots} / \texttt{fc3817b3\dots} for $P_1$/$P_2$/$P_3$/$P_4$). Two flux-domain gates ($P_F$, $F$-PDF) are evaluated directly on each checkpoint; the line-of-sight density axis is reported via the 1D-along-ray proxy (per-sightline Pearson $r$ on $\log_{10}\rho/\langle\rho\rangle$ at integrator quadrature points), a necessary-but-not-sufficient surrogate for the full 3D $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}(r)$.

\textbf{Rescale-as-preview, retracted.} The cost-survey rescale-as-preview interpretation is empirically falsified by the trained-at-target $P_F$ residual (overlay figure in suppl.): $4\times$ more training and the corrected anchor increased $|\Delta P_F/P_F|$ from $\PFrescaledPercent$ (rescaled preview) to $\PFresidPonePercent$ at $P_1$.

\textbf{Empirical observation and diagnosis: $P_F$ is structurally bound, not calibration-bound.} At the corrected anchor and full publication schedule (per-cell values in suppl.), the four-physics bundle at the seed=42 single-seed anchor achieves: $\langle F_{\text{pred}}\rangle \in [\meanFseedFortyTwoRangeLo, \meanFseedFortyTwoRangeHi]$ in $4/4$ cells (cross-physics spread $\meanFseedFortyTwoSpread$, $<\!1\%$ bar); KS$\,< \GateKS$ in $3/4$ cells (range $[\KSseedFortyTwoPone, \KSseedFortyTwoPtwo]$, $P_2$ the single breach); pre-registered safety rails $4/4$; \texttt{peak\_vram}$=\peakVRAMPubtone$ GB; \texttt{tau\_amp}$\in[\tauampPubtoneRangeLo, \tauampPubtoneRangeHi]$; and $|\Delta P_F/P_F| \in [\PFresidLo, \PFresidHi]$ over $k_\parallel \in [10^{\PFkLoExp}, 10^{\PFkHiExp}]$ s/km in $4/4$ cells, $\PFfactorOverGateLo\times$--$\PFfactorOverGateHi\times$ over the $\GatePFdec$ gate. The 5-seed sightline-level bootstrap returns $\langle F_{\rm pred}\rangle \in [\meanFBootstrapRangeLo, \meanFBootstrapRangeHi]$ (cross-physics spread $\meanFBootstrapSpread$, systematically shifted below the seed=42 anchor by $\meanFseedDeviationRangeLo$--$\meanFseedDeviationRangeHi\sigma$); KS closes $3/4$ bootstrap-robustly. The conjunction --- mean-flux closure at the single-seed anchor, near-uniform KS closure, and a uniformly $\sim\!4\times$-over $P_F$ residual --- is inconsistent with a calibration deficit (which would shift mean-flux without preserving the $\meanFBootstrapSpread$ cross-physics tightness) and with a $P_2$-only seed-collapse story (which would not produce $P_F$ residuals at the three non-collapsed cells). The MLP gap is in second-order spectroscopic statistics that survive matched mean-flux and matched flux PDF. The explicit-grid probe of \S\ref{sec:experiments:gridprobe} shows this gap is not an MLP-capacity limit: it persists at the maximal-capacity parameterization, where the under-constraint of the inverse problem is the operative cause.

\textbf{Decoupling finding.} Log-space $\tau$-MSE is not a sufficient proxy for the structure-gate spectroscopic statistics; see suppl.

Companion plots and per-cell tables are in the supplementary.

\subsection{$\tau$-binned residual decomposition: saturation-regime under-fitting, positively identified cross-physics}
\label{sec:experiments:tau-binned}
The structural-$P_F$ verdict of \S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval} states \emph{that} the gap is in second-order spectroscopic statistics and \emph{not} in calibration. The diagnostic question is \emph{where on the $F$ axis} the gap lives. We answer this with a $\tau$-binned residual decomposition (\texttt{scripts/tau\_binned\_residual.py --cell all}) over the four \texttt{pub-t1} corrected-anchor checkpoints at step $50{,}000$, seed $=42$, $1024$ rays. The diagnostic partitions the rendered $F$ axis into seven bins matching the saturated-absorber definition of \S\ref{sec:method:loss} --- linear regime $F > 0.9$ (bins 1--4, $\sim\!99.6\%$ of pixels), transition band, and saturation band $F \in [\SatBandLo, \SatBandHi]$ (bin 7) --- and reports the per-bin residual mass $\sum |\Delta F|$ alongside pixel share. The concentration ratio $R = (\text{residual-mass share}) / (\text{pixel-mass share})$ measures how much of the per-sightline error budget the saturation band absorbs relative to its size.

\textbf{Finding.} The saturation-band $R$ is $\{\RsatPone, \RsatPtwo, \RsatPthree, \RsatPfour\}$ across $P_1$/$P_2$/$P_3$/$P_4$ (per-cell breakdown and 4-panel residual-mass histograms in suppl.). All four cells exceed the positive-ID floor $R \geq \GateRsatFloor$ by $> \!\RsatFactorOverFloor\times$, the cross-physics spread is $\sim\!\RsatSpreadRelative$ relative, and $P_4$ (strongest-feedback variant, where cool dense structure is most prevalent) has the largest $R$. The linear regime contributes $|\langle \Delta F\rangle| \leq \RsatLinearBandResidual$ across all four cells, so the cross-physics flux residual budget is dominated by the saturation band rather than spread across the $F$ axis. Within the saturation band, the per-bin Pearson $r$ on $\tau$ between predicted and ground-truth values is $r_{\text{pearson}}(\tau) = \RsatPearsonTauPone$ at $P_1$: the $\tau$-rank order collapses where it matters most for $P_F$, ruling out a global $\tau$ rescaling (uniform $\tau \to s \cdot \tau$ preserves rank order in-band) as a sufficient fix.

\textbf{Interpretation under the falsification discipline.} The decomposition positively identifies the saturation regime as the residually-supported mechanism cross-physics. We do not claim closure of the inferential gap: the strict ``saturation under-fitting \emph{is the cause} of the $P_F$ gap'' claim is reserved for the successor counterfactual run (\S\ref{sec:nextsteps:pf}). The current claim ceiling is \emph{positively identified as the residually-supported mechanism cross-physics; counterfactual closure (saturation-aware loss) deferred to successor work}.

The full-resolution per-physics breakdown (4-panel residual-mass histograms with saturation-band shading) is provided as Fig.~3 of the supplementary. The per-cell $R$ values are tabulated there alongside the figure.

\subsection{Explicit-grid probe: the flux inverse problem is under-constrained}
\label{sec:experiments:gridprobe}
The MLP diagnosis of \S\ref{sec:experiments:cosmological-eval}--\S\ref{sec:experiments:tau-binned} localizes the residual but leaves one question open: is the saturation-band deficit a capacity limit of the implicit field, or a property of the inverse problem? We answer with the explicit-voxel-grid probe of \S\ref{sec:method:gridprobe} --- four free-per-voxel grids at $\GridG^3$, plain [D-24] flux supervision, one lever, at $P_1$, $z = 0.3$, $n_{\text{rays}} = 1024$ (Juno job 221335, $50{,}000$ steps).

\textbf{Trainability: the grid escapes the optimization collapse.} The grid's predicted-flux-power-band variance is $\VarPfBandRatio$, flat from step $5{,}000$ to $50{,}000$ --- about $1000\times$ over the collapse bar that retired the interventions of \S\ref{sec:nextsteps:pf}. The grid trains. Its variance sits at the same $\approx\!1.0$ level as the healthy MLP run, so it lands in the same saturation-band partial-underfit basin (Mode A) at the most-expressive parameterization; this is the representation-invariance of that basin landing, scoped to $P_F$.

\textbf{Decisive evidence (K2): the grid beats the truth on flux.} We forward-model the \emph{true} sightline fields ($\rho, T, X_{HI}, v_{\text{pec}}$ at the ray points) through the same integrator under the exact [D-24] loss --- RSD applied identically, amplitude $\tau_{\text{amp}}$ free --- and minimize over $\tau_{\text{amp}}$. The true field scores data loss $\KTwoTruthLoss$; the grid scores $\KTwoGridLoss$, about $\KTwoMarginFactor\times$ better. The grid fits the observed flux better than the true field does through the same forward model. The margin is amplitude-flat (the true-field loss is flat over $\tau_{\text{amp}} \in [0.2, 4]$), which rules out the amplitude-calibration escape; the non-trivial content is the $\KTwoMarginFactor\times$ margin and that flatness, not the bare $\text{grid} < \text{truth}$ inequality. The margin includes integrator-induced slack: the $\KTwoTruthLoss$ floor is our FGPA-vs-radiative-transfer integrator error, not a physical residual, and the grid can exploit slack the truth cannot. The reading is that the flux through this forward model does not identify the true 3D field --- the $z = 0.3$ flux inverse problem is under-constrained. We do not separate problem-intrinsic from forward-model-induced under-determination at $\langle F\rangle = \MeanFluxZpThree$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:k2-degeneracy}).

\textbf{Supporting evidence: weak 3D structure, ceiling-relative.} The grid's 3D density cross-correlation at $r = 2\,h^{-1}$ Mpc is $\XiGrid$. The $\GateXi$ acceptance bar is demoted on two grounds, both confirmed first-hand. First, the estimator returns a cross-correlation function that decays with $r$: truth-vs-truth scores $\XiCeiling$ at $r = 2\,h^{-1}$ Mpc, not $\approx\!1.0$, so no field can pass $\GateXi$ and the ``$\XiGrid$ vs.\ $\GateXi$'' reading is invalid. Read ceiling-relative, the grid recovers $\XiCeilingFraction$ of the field's own achievable $r = 2\,h^{-1}$ Mpc autocorrelation and sits below truth-plus-100\%-noise ($\XiTruthNoiseHundred$) --- weak structure recovery. Second, a real-space-cube-vs-redshift-space-flux frame mismatch confounds $\xi(r = 2)$: the estimator scores a real-space cube against real-space truth while supervision is redshift-space flux with no RSD remap, and at $z = 0.3$ the displacement is $\Delta\chi_{\text{rms}} = \FrameDeltaChiRms$, $\Delta\chi_{\text{p95}} = \FrameDeltaChiPNF\,h^{-1}$ Mpc, comparable to the $r = 2\,h^{-1}$ Mpc gate scale. The mismatch depresses $\xi(r = 2)$ independent of reconstruction quality, on both this neural number and the idealized Wiener baseline (lower bound $\WienerXiLowerBound$, optimum un-pinned). $\xi$ is therefore supporting, not load-bearing; K2 carries the close. A corrected per-lag coefficient is banked as future work (\S\ref{sec:nextsteps}). Fig.~\ref{fig:xi-ceiling} shows the $\xi(r)$ profiles ceiling-relative.

Escaping the optimization collapse is necessary but not sufficient: the flux through this integrator does not determine the 3D field. We make no claim beyond $z = 0.3$.

\subsection{Substrate-discriminability probe at the reconstruction scale}
\label{sec:experiments:substrate-probe}
The structure-gate readout (\S\ref{sec:experiments:headline}) and the $\tau$-binned residual decomposition (\S\ref{sec:experiments:tau-binned}) characterize the rendered $F$ axis. A separate question is what cross-physics signal the truth density field carries at the spatial scale a downstream 3D consumer would slice it on. We answer this with a probe-classifier construction~\cite{alain_bengio2017probing}: a 3D ResNet-18 (halved channels, $\SubstrateProbeParams$ parameters) is trained to assign $\SubstrateProbeCropSize^3$-voxel crops of the truth $\rho/\bar\rho$ field --- $\SubstrateProbeVoxelKpc$ kpc/h voxels, a $\SubstrateProbeBoxMpc\,h^{-1}$ Mpc cube edge, matched to the $\xi_{\hat\rho,\rho}$ evaluation scale of \S\ref{sec:experiments:headline} --- to one of the four Sherwood feedback variants $\{P_1, P_2, P_3, P_4\}$. The classifier accuracy is a lower bound on the cross-physics discriminability the substrate carries; the construction is read as a measurement of representational signal at this scale, not as a paper claim about Sherwood-recipe distinguishability per se (which is published in 1D flux statistics; see below).

The probe is paired with a two-scalar $[\text{mean}, \text{variance}]$ MLP baseline as a pre-registered anti-degeneracy floor: any signal the 3D ResNet exploits beyond the marginal one-point density distribution must clear a $\SubstrateProbeADfiveBarPP$ pp margin over this baseline (Geirhos et al.~\cite{geirhos2020shortcut} shortcut-learning audit).\footnote{The $\SubstrateProbeADfiveBarPP$ pp margin is a project-internal disciplinary threshold adopted for this analysis; we are not aware of an external observational anchor for this specific value. We adopt it by symmetry with the AdamW ablation precedent of Loshchilov \& Hutter~\cite{loshchilov2019adamw}, where sub-percentage-point accuracy differences are read as significant at benchmark-scale test sets ($n\!>\!50{,}000$), scaled to our $n\!\approx\!8{,}000$ Sherwood-crop test set.} Confidence intervals on $\hat A$ are reported in two forms --- ordinary $K{=}1{,}000$ resample bootstrap and a block bootstrap~\cite{politis_romano1994} at block size $\SubstrateProbeBlockVoxels$ voxels ($\sim\!2\times$ the gate scale) to respect the spatial autocorrelation of the underlying field. Evaluation is on $N_{\text{crops}} = \SubstrateProbeNcrops$ held-out crops drawn from an axis-0 70/15/15 spatial split.

\textbf{Result.} The 3D ResNet reaches $\hat A_{\text{overall}} = \SubstrateProbeAcc$ on the 4-way task; the two-scalar baseline reaches $\SubstrateProbeMeanVarAcc$, against a chance baseline of $\SubstrateProbeChance$. The margin is $\SubstrateProbeMarginPP$ pp, below the pre-registered $\SubstrateProbeADfiveBarPP$ pp anti-degeneracy floor, and we therefore decline to publish $\hat A_{\text{overall}}$ as a primary accuracy number for the substrate. Block-bootstrap and ordinary CIs on $\hat A_{\text{overall}}$ are $[\SubstrateProbeCIblockLo, \SubstrateProbeCIblockHi]$ and $[\SubstrateProbeCIordLo, \SubstrateProbeCIordHi]$ respectively (maximum CI-lower-bound difference $0.004$, $\ll 0.02$; the block correction does not change the conclusion). Per-quintile accuracies on the val-set-fixed equal-occupancy distance bins are $\hat A(r_{25}) = \SubstrateProbeAccQone$, $\hat A(r_{50}) = \SubstrateProbeAccQtwo$, $\hat A(r_{75}) = \SubstrateProbeAccQthree$ --- a flat curve with cross-quintile range $\sim\!\SubstrateProbeAccRangePP$ pp. The confusion matrix concentrates diagonal mass on the $P_1$ and $P_4$ rows, with the $P_2$/$P_3$ rows showing near-chance off-diagonal spread: the extremes (no feedback vs.\ strong AGN) show higher diagonal mass than the middle (stellar wind vs.\ wind$+$AGN) at this substrate.

\textbf{Prior-art context.} Cross-physics discriminability of the Sherwood $\{P_1$--$P_4\}$ variants is established at the 1D Ly$\alpha$-forest flux-statistic scale by the suite-overview comparisons in Bolton et al.~\cite{bolton2017sherwood} and by the cross-physics $P_F$ differentials reported in Iršič et al.~\cite{irsic2017xq100}, cited here as precedent for the variants' physical separability at the 1D flux-statistic scale. On this probe, at the $\SubstrateProbeCropSize^3$ density-crop substrate matched to the gate scale and at the single resolution tested, the discriminative content the 3D ResNet recovers does not exceed what two summary moments capture by more than $\SubstrateProbeMarginPP$ pp; the probe at this substrate configuration does not, on this measurement, surface higher-order signal beyond two summary moments. A successor probe at a different substrate scale or on a different physical field is the natural follow-on (\S\ref{sec:nextsteps}).

\subsection{Open evaluation axes and compute footprint}
